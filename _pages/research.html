Customer Churn Management: A Machine Learning Perspective

Batchelor's thesis under supervision of Prof. Emmanouil E. Zachariadis in collaboration with Vodafone Greece

Abstract

Within a saturated market, customer acquisition no longer ensures sustainable revenue. The paradigm has moved towards customer retention. Churn management is a major problem and one of the most important concerns for large companies. Due to the direct effect on the revenues, companies have realized that waiting for a cancellation request to act, is not an efficient strategy. Instead, they should take into account the risk of churn and step in before customers make that decision. The most promising way is through Machine Learning, considering the customer churn problem as a classification problem of high-risk churners and non-churners.

My thesis aims to provide a literature review on Machine Learning, the widespread methods and techniques for its application to classification problems, with an emphasis on the churn management. More specifically, the research questions that the thesis attempts to answer are the following:

  1. What are the main Machine Learning techniques for classification problems?
  2. How Machine Learning powered churn analysis for modern day businesses?

In addition, a particular methodology of a churn prediction model is proposed and implemented in the context of an internship. The final report of my thesis can be found here.
